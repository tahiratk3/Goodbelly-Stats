# GoodBelly Marketing Effectiveness Analysis

## Overview

This project evaluates the effectiveness of GoodBelly's marketing initiatives using statistical modeling and regression analysis. The objective was to determine whether promotional activities such as in-store demos and endcap displays generate a measurable increase in product sales and justify their marketing costs.

## Business Problem

GoodBelly leadership questioned whether in-store demonstrations and endcap displays produced enough incremental sales to warrant continued investment. The goal was to quantify the impact of these promotional programs and provide data-driven recommendations.

## Methodology

- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Exploratory data analysis
- Correlation analysis to detect multicollinearity
- Multiple linear regression modeling
- Variable selection using statistical significance testing
- Model validation through residual analysis

### Variables Analyzed

- Product Price
- Sales Representative Presence
- Endcap Displays
- In-Store Demonstrations
- Lagged Demo Effects
- Number of SKUs
- Store Characteristics

## Key Findings

- Endcap displays generated approximately 305 additional units sold per week.
- In-store demos increased sales by approximately 120 units during the promotion week.
- Demo effects persisted:
  - +68 units for 1–3 weeks after demo
  - +49 units for 4+ weeks after demo
- Sales representatives contributed approximately 80 additional weekly units.
- A $1 price increase reduced sales by approximately 24 units.

## Results

- Final model explained approximately 62% of sales variation.
- All retained variables were statistically significant.
- Results supported continued investment in demos and endcap promotions.

## Tools Used

- Excel
- Statistical Regression Analysis
- Data Cleaning & Transformation

## Business Impact

The analysis provided quantitative evidence supporting continued funding for GoodBelly's promotional programs and identified endcap displays as the highest ROI marketing initiative.
